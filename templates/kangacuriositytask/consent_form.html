PURPOSE:
We are conducting a psychology research study on learning. We want to investigate how people learn, what helps them to learn most effectively, and how different psychological processes affect learning. To participate in this survey, you must be at least 18 years old.

PROCEDURE:
If you choose to participate, you will view videos, questions, images, or pieces of information and asked to answer some questions. There may also be some basic questions about your preferences, mood, and personality. You may skip any question that you feel uncomfortable answering. It should take approximately 30 minutes to complete this task.

RISKS:
There are no known physical, psychological, or social risks associated with this study. You may skip any questions that make you uncomfortable, and you may quit the survey at any time if you don’t want to continue.

BENEFITS:
There are no direct benefits to you for participating in this survey. However, the research may help advance scientific knowledge of learning.

ALTERNATIVE PROCEDURES:
The alternative is to not participate.

CONFIDENTIALITY:
All of your answers will be completely anonymous, and we will not ask for any piece of information that could be used to identify you. We will not collect IP addresses that could be linked to you through your computer. The following individuals and/or agencies will be able to look at and copy your research records:

  • The investigator, study staff, and other professionals who may be evaluating the study
  • Authorities from Columbia University, including the Institutional Review Board (‘IRB’)
  • The United States Office of Human Research Protections (‘OHRP’)

COMPENSATION:
You will receive $1.00-$3.00 for this survey, depending on what is specified in the HIT description. Once you have completed the survey, a researcher will review and approve payment, and the money will be transferred into your MTurk account within 48 hours.

ADDITIONAL COSTS:
It will not cost you anything to participate in this study.

VOLUNTARY PARTICIPATION:
Taking part in this study is your choice. You may refuse to take part in all or some portion of it, and you may exit the survey at any time.
